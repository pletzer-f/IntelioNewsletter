ECB holds benchmark rate at 2.75% — signals openness to Q2 easing as disinflation remains firmly on track

The European Central Bank left its deposit facility rate unchanged for the third consecutive meeting, with President Lagarde citing "growing confidence" in returning inflation to the 2% target by mid-year.

Governing Council members indicated that March CPI data will be decisive for a potential April cut. Markets are now pricing in 65 basis points of easing over 2026, with the first full cut expected by June. Core inflation fell to 2.6% in January from 2.8% in December — the third consecutive monthly decline — reinforcing the gradual disinflation narrative across the eurozone.

The March data will effectively cast the deciding vote.

For fixed income markets, the short end of the German Bund curve has already priced in two full cuts by September 2026. Spread compression in periphery bonds (Italy, Spain) reflects growing confidence in the easing cycle, with BTPs trading at their tightest spread to Bunds since early 2022.

EU announces €12bn accelerated grid expansion framework — Austria and Germany named priority corridors through 2028

The European Commission unveiled a fast-tracked transmission infrastructure plan, cutting permitting timelines from seven years to under three for priority cross-border grid projects in the DACH region and Nordics.

The framework targets 8,500km of new high-voltage transmission lines, with Austrian grid operator Verbund and Germany's 50Hertz as lead implementers. Projects qualify for EU Connecting Europe Facility co-financing at up to 50% of eligible capex, with first tender rounds expected in Q3 2026.

The European Commission's grid framework is structurally different from previous infrastructure initiatives in one critical respect: it designates specific "Priority Project Corridors" where member states are legally obligated to accelerate permitting. For the Austria–Germany corridor, this means environmental impact assessments will be conducted in parallel with planning rather than sequentially — the primary driver of the timeline compression from seven to under three years.

"This is not an aspiration — it is a binding directive. Member states that fail to meet permitting timelines will face infringement proceedings." — EU Energy Commissioner · Brussels press conference, 25 Feb 2026

Verbund has confirmed it will lead the Austrian corridor as primary transmission system operator, with a preliminary capex budget of €2.8bn for the Austrian segments alone. First tenders — focused on substation upgrades and cable supply — are expected in Q3 2026. For businesses in engineering, construction, electrical equipment, or project finance, the procurement pipeline will be among the largest in Austria this decade. Registration as a Verbund approved supplier is the critical first step; the vendor qualification process typically takes 4–6 months.

Large-scale grid projects typically generate 3–5× ancillary procurement volume — a significant opportunity for industrial, electrical, and infrastructure businesses. Begin positioning with Verbund and 50Hertz procurement teams in H1 2026 ahead of expected Q3 tender launches.

Vienna logistics vacancy hits five-year low at 2.8% — rents up 6.2% YoY

E-commerce growth and supply chain near-shoring drove sustained demand for last-mile and urban logistics assets in the Vienna basin. Developer pipeline remains constrained by land scarcity and permitting delays, supporting further rent appreciation.

Prime Vienna logistics rents now stand at €8.40/m²/month — up from €7.90 a year ago and €6.20 in early 2023. The most acute shortage is in the 5,000–15,000m² segment, where take-up in 2025 exceeded new supply by a factor of 3.1×. Three speculative development schemes that broke ground in 2024 have all been pre-leased before completion, reflecting the depth of occupier demand.

The constrained supply side is structural: Vienna's industrial zoning is highly restrictive, with few sites larger than 5ha available in the eastern logistics corridor (Schwechat–Lanzendorf–Fischamend). Development lead times from land identification to occupancy average 3.8 years in this market. For occupiers, this means accepting longer commitment periods (7–10 year leases with limited break options) as the price of securing space — a significant operational planning consideration.

Businesses with Vienna logistics lease renewals due in 2026 should initiate negotiations immediately — landlords are in a strong position. Consider 5-year+ terms to lock in before further rent appreciation. If you hold logistics assets, current market conditions represent a strong sale window.
